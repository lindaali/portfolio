— INTRODUCTION —

In the summer of 2018, I had the exciting opportunity to intern at Premier Paint Roller LLC with the Mechanical Process Engineering Team. The following is a collection of projects I worked on during this time.

— BRUSH TIPPER —

The tipper machine is one of the main projects I worked on during this summer. It is the machine that sands and cards the brush filaments that results in softer and higher quality brushes. For this, I drafted drawings for worn out and spare components. This included shafts, guardings, and bearing supports. In addition, I changed some of the components on the machine to make the machine more efficient and produce higher quality results. Lastly, I standardized the production process on the machine and created a troubleshooting protocol document.

— PRESS GUIDES —

For the printing press that labels the handles after manufacturing, I designed guides to ensure that the handle would be in the same position for printing every time. This included a stationary component to keep the handle straight and two sliding components too accommodate multiple handle sizes.

— NAILING EXTENSION —

The nailing machine is one of the final machines used to make brush and nails finished brush heads and handles together. For this machine, there was no real support for the handle as the operator was nailing the brush head in. This caused the brush head to be crooked in some cases and thus, not marketable. To solve this, I designed a table extension that would integrate with the machine for the operator's use.

— MISCELLANEOUS —

In additon to the previous projects mentioned, I created a highly detailed model of one of the smaller machines. This was so we could manufacture spare parts rather than buying new components. Lastly, I completed one of the previous interns project by revising and assembling their device.
